Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, Raipur was established in July 1987 in pursuance to the National Policy of Education 1986. The objective of the vidyalaya is to provide free education of highly quality to the talented students of the distinct, hailing from rural areas. Students are selected on a competitive selection test conducted by the Central Board of Secondary Education, New Delhi. Selected students are provided free boarding facilities, text books, stationery etc. The admitted children are accommodated in different houses. The effective management of a residential School depends upon the quality of the house system fuctioning of the school. A house consists of children group effectively led by the (House Master/house mistress) who act as a parent, guide, philosopher and friend for every member of the house when the child is admitted in the vidyalaya he/she will be immediately admitting into a house (There are 4 houses Aravali, Nilgiri, Shivalik, Udaigiri) . The houses will create a feeling of oneness not only among the vidyalaya but also among the children who migrate in class IX from one linguistic region to another.
